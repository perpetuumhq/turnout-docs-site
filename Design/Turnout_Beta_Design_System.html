Turnout Design System

The complete specification for Turnout.so — a venue demand operating system for India's go-out economy. Warm, competent, mobile-first, category-adaptive, and persona-responsive.

Design Principles

Every design decision at Turnout is guided by three core principles that shape our tone, navigation, and component behavior.

Warm but Competent

We're friendly and approachable, but never unprofessional. Trust and clarity come first. We use conversational language, embrace contractions, and make every interaction feel like a conversation with a knowledgeable friend.

Outcome-First

Every screen, button, and message leads to a real outcome. We highlight what happens when users act, not just what to do. No feature-first language. No jargon. No passive voice.

WhatsApp-Native Simplicity

Our primary CTA is WhatsApp. We design for mobile-first (375px). We use simple language, short sentences, and patterns users know from messaging apps. One emoji per header, maximum.

Key Design Priorities

  • Mobile-first (375px is the primary target)
  • Category-adaptive (Bars differ from Fitness Studios)
  • Persona-responsive (Solo Explorer ≠ Group Organizer)
  • Color ratio: 60% white, 25% navy, 10% orange, 5% extended
  • Trust-heavy (Ratings, reviews, credentials always visible)

Spacing & Grid

All measurements are divisible by 4px. This creates a consistent rhythm throughout the system and ensures pixel-perfect alignment across devices.

Responsive Behavior

Turnout is mobile-first. Design for 375px, then enhance for tablet and desktop.

Breakpoint Width Layout Shift Primary Use
Mobile 375px Single column, full-width cards Primary design target
Landscape Mobile 640px Still single column, wider padding Mobile landscape mode
Tablet 768px 2-column grid, reduced gaps iPad portrait
Tablet Landscape 1024px 2-3 column grid, larger gaps iPad landscape, small desktop

Responsive Patterns

Buttons

  • Mobile: Full-width in columns
  • Desktop: Inline, 160px min
  • Spacing: 12px gap always

Cards

  • Mobile: 1 column, 12px gap
  • Tablet: 2 columns, 24px gap
  • Desktop: 3 columns, 32px gap

Images

  • Mobile: Full-width minus padding
  • Desktop: Constrained to max-width
  • Radius: 8px (gallery) to 14px (feature)

Navigation

  • Mobile: Hamburger (hidden nav)
  • Tablet: Tab pills visible
  • Desktop: Full nav bar

Animation & Interaction

Subtle, purposeful animations guide attention and provide feedback. All transitions feel snappy and warm.

Page Transitions

Fade-in 0.3s ease when navigating between pages. Creates smooth flow without distraction.

Card Hover

translateY(-2px) + shadow increase. Signals interactivity without being dramatic.

CTA Hover

Darken 10%, scale(1.02). Confirms action is ready. Feels responsive.

Scroll Reveal

Opacity 0→1, translateY(12px→0), staggered 80ms per item. Draws eye down the page.

Loading States

Orange spinner, 2px stroke, 24px diameter. Consistent with brand warmth.

Timing

All transitions 200-300ms. Instant feels clinical. 500ms+ feels slow.

Accessibility

Turnout is designed for everyone. Clear contrast, keyboard navigation, and touch targets make our interfaces usable for all.

Color Contrast

Color Combo Ratio Use Case WCAG Level
#FF6B35 on #FFFFFF (Orange on white) 3.2:1 Large text only (18px+) AA
#C44A1C on #FFFFFF (Dark Orange on white) 7.1:1 Body text, small text AAA
#0F172A on #FFFFFF (Navy on white) 16.7:1 All text sizes AAA
#25D366 on #FFFFFF (WhatsApp Green on white) 5.1:1 Body text, UI elements AA

Touch Targets & Focus States

  • Minimum touch target: 44px × 44px (buttons, links, inputs)
  • Recommended spacing: 8px gap between touch targets
  • Focus state: 2px orange outline, 2px offset
  • Keyboard navigation: Tab order logical, visible focus always
  • Link underlines: Links underlined or color-contrasted ≥3:1

Mobile Accessibility

Text Sizing

  • Body text: min 14px on mobile
  • Headings: min 18px
  • Captions: min 12px
  • Allow user zoom up to 200%

Form Inputs

  • 44px minimum height
  • Labels visible + connected
  • Error messages clear & actionable
  • Autocomplete attributes set

Semantic HTML

  • Use <button> for buttons, <a> for links
  • Heading hierarchy h1→h6 (not purely stylistic)
  • Use <section>, <nav>, <footer> for structure
  • Alt text on all images. Decorative images marked alt=""
  • Form labels explicitly connected with <label for="id">

Brand Voice in UI

Every word on Turnout reflects our personality. Warm, conversational, outcome-first.

Voice Principles

Conversational

Use contractions. Write like you're texting a friend, not a manual.

✓ "Let's find your vibe"
✗ "Locate your preferred experience"

Warm

Approachable, human, friendly. Never robotic or corporate.

✓ "You're gonna love this place"
✗ "This establishment scores high"

Outcome-First

Lead with what happens, not what to do. "Get in" not "book now."

✓ "Get in for 2 on Friday at 6"
✗ "Click the button to reserve"

Clear

No jargon, no passive voice, no feature-first language.

✓ "Save 20% this weekend"
✗ "Dynamic pricing optimization available"

Emoji Usage

Max 1 emoji per header. Use them to reinforce, not replace, meaning.

✓ "🍻 Bars & Pubs"
✗ "Bars & Pubs 🍺🍻🥃🍸"

Specificity

Numbers, times, specifics. Builds trust and reduces friction.

✓ "4 seats left for 8 PM Friday"
✗ "Limited availability"
